## How Car Loan Interest Rates Work

### Simple Interest vs Reducing Balance

**Reducing Balance (Most Common)**
- Interest charged on remaining balance only
- As you pay down the loan, interest reduces
- This is how most Australian car loans work

**Example:** $30,000 loan at 7% over 5 years
- Month 1 interest: $175 (7% ÷ 12 × $30,000)
- Month 60 interest: $3.50 (7% ÷ 12 × $600)

**Fixed Interest (Less Common)**
- Interest calculated on original amount for entire term
- You pay more overall
- Sometimes used by dealer finance

## What Affects Your Car Loan Interest Rate?

### 1. Credit Score (Biggest Factor)
Your credit score is the #1 factor determining your rate.

| Score Range | Rating | Rate Impact |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 800-1000 | Excellent | Best rates available |
| 700-799 | Very Good | Near-best rates |
| 625-699 | Good | Competitive rates |
| 550-624 | Fair | Higher rates, fewer options |
| 0-549 | Poor | Limited options, high rates |

**Pro tip:** Check your credit score for free at Credit Savvy or ClearScore before applying.

### 2. Secured vs Unsecured

**Secured Car Loan**
- Car used as security (collateral)
- Lower rates (5.99% - 15%)
- Lender can repossess if you default
- Best for lowest overall cost

**Unsecured Personal Loan**
- No security required
- Higher rates (8.99% - 24%)
- More flexibility
- Good for older cars or if you want to modify the vehicle

### 3. Loan Term Length
| Term | Rate Impact | Monthly Payment | Total Interest |
|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 3 years | Lowest rates | Highest | Lowest |
| 5 years | Standard rates | Medium | Medium |
| 7 years | Highest rates | Lowest | Highest |

**Example on $30,000 loan at 7.99%:**
- 3 years: $940/month, $3,840 total interest
- 5 years: $608/month, $6,480 total interest
- 7 years: $468/month, $9,312 total interest

### 4. Vehicle Age and Type
- **New cars:** Best rates
- **Used cars (< 5 years):** Slightly higher rates
- **Used cars (5-10 years):** Higher rates, some lenders decline
- **Used cars (> 10 years):** Limited lenders, highest rates

### 5. Loan Amount
- **Under $10,000:** Some lenders have minimum amounts; rates may be higher
- **$10,000 - $50,000:** Standard rates
- **Over $50,000:** May qualify for premium rates

### 6. Employment Type
| Employment | Rate Impact |
|------------|-------------|
| Permanent full-time | Best rates |
| Permanent part-time | Good rates |
| Casual (12+ months) | Standard rates |
| Self-employed (2+ years ABN) | Standard rates |
| Newly employed (<6 months) | Higher rates or decline |

## Fixed vs Variable Car Loan Rates

### Fixed Rate
✅ **Pros:**
- Repayments never change
- Protected if rates rise
- Easier budgeting

❌ **Cons:**
- Won't benefit if rates fall
- Break costs if you pay out early
- Usually slightly higher than variable

### Variable Rate
✅ **Pros:**
- May start lower than fixed
- Benefit if rates fall
- Often no early payout penalty

❌ **Cons:**
- Repayments can increase
- Harder to budget
- Less predictable

**Our recommendation:** Fixed rate for car loans. The certainty is worth any small premium, especially when rates could go either way.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a good car loan interest rate in Australia?
A good rate depends on your circumstances, but generally:
- **Excellent:** Under 7%
- **Good:** 7% - 9%
- **Average:** 9% - 12%
- **High:** Over 12%

If you're paying more than 9-10% with decent credit, you're likely overpaying.

### Can I negotiate car loan interest rates?
Yes. Banks and lenders have discretion, especially for customers with good credit. A broker can negotiate on your behalf across multiple lenders simultaneously.

### Does paying off a car loan early hurt my credit?
No, paying off a loan early is positive for your credit. However, some lenders charge early exit fees — check your contract.

### Should I get finance through the dealer or a broker?
Almost always go with a broker or arrange finance independently. Dealer finance is convenient but typically 2-5% more expensive.

### How do I refinance to a lower interest rate?
If you're currently on a high rate, refinancing is simple:

2. Get quotes from other lenders (Nik Finance compares 130+ free)
3. Apply for the new loan
4. New lender pays out old loan
5. Start paying the lower rate

## The Hidden Costs to Watch For

### Establishment/Application Fees
- Range: $0 - $500
- Included in comparison rate

### Monthly Account Keeping Fees
- Range: $0 - $15/month
- Included in comparison rate

### Early Exit Fees
- Range: $0 - $500+
- NOT included in comparison rate
- Check before signing

### Late Payment Fees
- Range: $15 - $50 per instance
- Can also hurt your credit score

### Balloon Payment (Residual)
- Large lump sum at end of loan
- Lowers monthly payments but you still owe it
- Must refinance or pay cash at end

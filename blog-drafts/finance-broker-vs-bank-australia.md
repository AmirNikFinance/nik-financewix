## How Brokers Get Paid

Understanding broker compensation:

### Upfront Commission
Lenders pay brokers a percentage of the loan amount when the loan settles (typically 0.5-0.7% for mortgages).

### Trail Commission
Ongoing small percentage while the loan remains active (typically 0.15-0.2% annually for mortgages).

### Why This Matters
- You don't pay directly
- Commission is the same regardless of which lender
- Good brokers recommend the best product, not the highest commission
- Trail commission incentivises ongoing service

## Common Broker Myths Debunked

### Myth 1: "Brokers push loans that pay them more"
**Reality:** Commissions are similar across lenders. Good brokers recommend based on fit, not commission.

### Myth 2: "Banks offer better rates directly"
**Reality:** Brokers often access exclusive rates. Banks rarely beat broker-sourced deals.

### Myth 3: "Using a broker takes longer"
**Reality:** Brokers streamline the process. They know exactly what each lender needs.

### Myth 4: "I have to pay the broker"
**Reality:** Brokers are free for most borrowers (paid by lenders).

### Myth 5: "My bank knows me best"
**Reality:** Your bank knows their products. A broker knows the entire market.

## What is Car Loan Refinancing?

Car loan refinancing means replacing your current car loan with a new one, typically at a lower interest rate. This can save you thousands of dollars over the life of your loan and reduce your monthly repayments.

If you're still paying dealer finance rates or took out a loan when rates were higher, you could be overpaying by thousands each year.

## Can I Refinance with Bad Credit?

Yes, but your options may be limited. Specialist lenders work with Australians who have:

- Previous bankruptcies

## Can I Refinance and Borrow Extra?

In some cases, yes. If your car's value exceeds your loan balance, you may be able to:
- Borrow additional funds for repairs or upgrades
- Access equity for other purposes
- Consolidate other debts

This is called a "cash-out refinance" and depends on your car's value and your borrowing capacity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does car loan refinancing take?
Typically 3-7 business days from application to settlement. Pre-approval can happen in 24 hours.

### Will refinancing hurt my credit score?
The initial credit check may cause a small, temporary dip. However, making on-time payments on your new loan will improve your score over time.

### Can I refinance a car loan with negative equity?
It's difficult but possible with some lenders. Negative equity means you owe more than the car is worth. You may need to pay down the difference or find a lender willing to work with you.

### Is there a minimum amount to refinance?
Most lenders have minimum loan amounts of $5,000-$10,000 for refinancing.

### Can I refinance if I'm self-employed?
Yes. Self-employed borrowers can refinance with the right documentation (tax returns, BAS, accountant letter).
